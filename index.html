Niina Glerean

Senior Lecturer · Laurea University of Applied Sciences PhD · University of Turku, Nursing Science (2025)

Niina Glerean is a nursing educator and researcher focusing on how young people and applicants perceive the nursing profession, and how evidence-informed student selection and digital interventions can support more accurate career understanding. Her work spans integrative review, qualitative research, psychometric instrument development (PNPI), and a 360° virtual learning environment intervention study.

Research

Research Focus & Methods

Career perception in nursing Student selection evidence base Psychometrics & IRT / Rasch 360° Virtual Learning Environments Nursing workforce retention Digital educational interventions

Glerean's research programme addresses a critical question in health workforce planning: why do young people choose — or not choose — nursing? Starting from an integrative review of adolescent perceptions, the programme moved to qualitative applicant research, instrument development (the PNPI, evaluated with Item Response Theory / partial credit model), and a quasi-experimental intervention using a 360° virtual learning environment within Finland's nursing entrance-exam context. Methodologically the work combines systematic synthesis, focus-group qualitative inquiry, mixed-methods psychometric testing, and experimental evaluation with propensity-score matching. All analysis code is publicly shared via GitHub.

Publications

Publications & Scholarly Outputs

Peer-reviewed journal articles
Promoting Correct Perception of the nursing Profession via 360° Virtual Learning Environment
Glerean N, Talman K, Glerean E, Hupli M, Haavisto E
Teaching and Learning in Nursing · 2025 · Vol. 20(2), e542–e549
✦ Open Access ⌥ Code: eglerean/sulo-intervention DOI 10.1016/j.teln.2025.01.004
View article → PDF (UTUPub) → Analysis code →
The study develops a 360° virtual learning environment (360°VLE) intended to provide realistic, neutral information about nursing to applicants, and tests its effect within the Finnish nursing entrance-exam process using a quasi-experimental design. Applicants in the intervention group received access to the platform alongside standard exam preparation materials; perception was measured using the validated PNPI instrument and analysed using multiple linear regression and propensity-score matching. Findings report a statistically significant improvement in applicants' perceptions of nursing when using the 360°VLE. Previous work history in healthcare and actively seeking information from nurses were also associated with more accurate perceptions. Supplementary materials and analysis code are openly available.
Development and psychometric testing of the perception of nursing profession instrument
Glerean N, Talman K, Glerean E, Hupli M, Haavisto E
Journal of Advanced Nursing · 2023 · Vol. 79(10), 4074–4087
✦ Open Access (CC BY) ⌥ Code: eglerean/sulo DOI 10.1111/jan.15726
View article → PDF (Aalto repository) → Analysis code →
The paper reports a mixed-methods instrument-development programme (2016–2022) that produced the PNPI: a 40-item instrument with four subscales covering the content of nursing work, career in nursing, the nature of nursing work, and the characteristics of a nurse. Item content validity was established via an expert panel. Psychometric evaluation used Item Response Theory (partial credit model), finding acceptable unidimensionality and item fit, but a mismatch between item difficulty and applicant ability — implying that further development should increase item difficulty to improve measurement accuracy in student-selection settings. R and Python code for all analyses are shared openly via GitHub; raw data cannot be shared owing to the protected nature of entrance-exam materials.
Perception of nursing profession – focus group interview among applicants to nursing education
Glerean N, Hupli M, Talman K, Haavisto E
Scandinavian Journal of Caring Sciences · 2019 · Vol. 33(2), 390–399
DOI 10.1111/scs.12635
View article →
Using focus group interviews with nursing applicants, the study explores the perceptions of nursing and the factors shaping those perceptions. It identifies three organising frames through which applicants understand nursing: (1) the characteristics expected of a nurse, (2) the content and nature of nursing work, and (3) career possibilities. Perceptions were influenced by personal factors, family and friends, media portrayals, and school career guidance. The study concludes that applicants' perceptions are often one-sided and partly unrealistic, motivating the need for targeted interventions addressing the public image of nursing in order to support informed career choice and long-term workforce retention.
Young peoples' perceptions of the nursing profession: An integrative review
Glerean N, Hupli M, Talman K, Haavisto E
Nurse Education Today · 2017 · Vol. 57, 95–102
DOI 10.1016/j.nedt.2017.07.008
View article →
This integrative review synthesises international research published between 2006 and 2016 on how adolescents and school-age young people perceive the nursing profession. The review finds persistent stereotypes and incomplete awareness of nursing education requirements and career pathways among young people. Influences on perceptions include family attitudes, peer opinions, media representations, and personal experiences. The review argues that more realistic, balanced information is needed in school career guidance and public communication to support informed recruitment decisions and improve the long-term attractiveness of nursing as a career.
